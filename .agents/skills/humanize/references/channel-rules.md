# Channel-Specific Rules

Writing rules and hard constraints by channel. Load the relevant section for
the channel the user has specified (or inferred from context). Apply all
constraints in that section before returning output.

---

## LinkedIn (Posts and Articles)

**Goal:** Direct, personal, specific. Reads like a person -- not a content
calendar.

**Structure:**
- One idea per post. Do not try to say two things.
- One sentence per line. No exceptions. No multi-sentence paragraphs.
- First line must hook without bait-and-click. No "I never expected this..." or "Here is why most people get X wrong..."
- No em dashes mid-sentence
- Subheadings only in articles, not posts

**Voice:**
- First-person opinions are expected and add credibility
- Specific numbers and outcomes outperform vague claims ("We hired 3 people in 2 weeks" vs "We scaled quickly")
- Personal stories need one concrete detail to feel real

**Hard Limits:**
- Post: 3,000 characters (flag if exceeded)
- Article: No hard limit, but opening paragraph must earn the read

**Do Not:**
- Hashtag walls at the end
- "Thoughts?" as a solo closing line
- "Excited to announce..." opener
- Bold every other phrase

## Email (Professional)

**Goal:** Clear, direct, purposeful. Respects the reader's time.

**Structure:**
- Subject line: specific and scannable. "Follow-up" is not a subject. Name the actual topic.
- Opening: no "I hope this finds you well" or "Happy Monday"
- State the ask or purpose in the first two sentences
- One ask per email. If there are multiple, number them clearly.
- Closing: no "Please don't hesitate to reach out" -- replace with specific instruction if needed: "Reply by Friday if you're in."

**Voice:**
- Professional does not mean cold. Contractions are fine.
- Shorter is better. If it can be 3 sentences, make it 3 sentences.

**Hard Limits:**
- No enforced character limit, but ideal length is under 150 words for requests and updates

**Do Not:**
- "Per my last email..."
- "As previously discussed..."
- "Looping in [Name] for visibility" without explaining why
- Padding sign-offs

## Blog / Long-Form

**Goal:** The writer's perspective is present and the reader finishes feeling
something changed.

**Structure:**
- Opening paragraph earns the read. No preamble, no scene-setting for its own sake.
- Subheadings are navigational, not decorative. A reader should be able to scan headings and understand the arc.
- Each paragraph has one job. If it has two, split it.
- The last paragraph is the conclusion -- do not add a section called "Conclusion"

**Voice:**
- The writer's perspective should be present and consistent throughout
- Does not need to be "balanced" -- have a stance
- Personal anecdotes need one specific detail to land

**Hard Limits:**
- No enforced length, but check that every paragraph earns its place

**Do Not:**
- "In this post, I'll cover..."
- "Stay tuned for part 2"
- Bullet lists as a substitute for argument

## Twitter / X (Posts and Threads)

**Goal:** One complete thought, fast.

**Structure:**
- Single post: one idea, full stop
- Thread: each post stands alone and advances the argument
- No mid-sentence line breaks for visual effect

**Voice:**
- Direct, opinionated, specific
- Dry wit is more effective than enthusiasm
- Do not soften claims. Have a position.

**Hard Limits:**
- Single post: 280 characters (flag if exceeded)
- Thread posts: each post 280 characters max

**Do Not:**
- "A thread:"
- Numbering every tweet "1/n" in the text (the thread format handles this)
- Ending with "RT if you agree"

## Slack / Internal Messaging

**Goal:** Get the information across as fast as possible. Respect async time.

**Structure:**
- Lead with the ask or update. Context after if needed.
- Sentence fragments are fine
- One idea per message unless it is a formal update

**Voice:**
- Emoji are fine if it fits the team culture
- Skip pleasantries unless it is a cold first-contact
- Match the energy of the channel -- a casual team channel is different from a cross-functional standup channel

**Hard Limits:**
- No enforced limit but anything over 5 lines should be a formatted message (bold headers, bullets) or a document

**Do Not:**
- Write a 200-word slack message that should be an email
- Use @here or @channel without a genuine broadcast need

---

## Internal Memo / Announcement

**Goal:** Inform the right people about a decision, update, or process change
with enough context to act.

**Structure:**
- Open with the key change or decision in the first sentence
- Follow with: what changed, why, what people need to do differently, when
- Background and context come after the action items -- not before

**Voice:**
- Formal but not bureaucratic
- Use "we" for company decisions, direct attribution for individual choices
- Passive voice is especially damaging here -- name who made the decision

**Do Not:**
- Bury the change in paragraph 3
- "As you may know..." (if they knew, you would not be sending this)
- "This is an exciting time for [Company]"
